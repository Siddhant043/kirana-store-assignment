# In-process scheduler; time-driven jobs deliver to the owner

Recurring work (auto-sent weekly analysis deck, daily khata-reminder scan) runs on an `AsyncIOScheduler` (APScheduler) inside the single always-on bot process — no separate worker or OS cron, one deployable. Schedule times live in owner preferences (reusing the cross-session memory system, e.g. "send my weekly deck Monday 9am IST"). Jobs are idempotent and DB-driven: a reminder digest records what it last sent so a mid-day restart doesn't double-send. Delivery is **owner-directed** — khata reminders and the weekly deck are pushed to the owner's Telegram chat, never to customers (customers are not users of the bot); this is the deliberate boundary. Because the bot is a single process, no distributed locking is needed. Misfire handling is best-effort with a small grace time: if the process is down when a job is due, that firing is missed and the owner can re-request the deck on demand — acceptable for v1 rather than adding a persistent catch-up jobstore.
